Presentation of +Kaos - a book on the first 10 years of Autistici/Inventati's experience
--:--

Autistici/Inventati (A/I) is an italian hacktivist collective that's been around for almost two decades now, offering free services like email, web hosting mailing lists and more to a passionate community of activists and privacy-aware users.

Some years ago Autistici/Inventati published a book in Italian on the first 10 years of their experience. Now that book is finally available in English!

We want to present it to our comrades and friends as widely as possible, and we would be extremely pleased to start our tour in Amsterdam, the setting of some of our crazy hacking lore.

During this presentation, we will recall the early years of our history, where hacking and grassroots movements produced a mixture ranging from Indymedia to hacking collectives like Autistici/Inventati (and not only) in Italy or Riseup (and not only) worldwide. After this historical introduction, we would like to discuss together with other participants the new scenarios of these recent days, where most of the dystopias of the cyberpunk novels we greedily read at the time of A/I's foundation appear more and more like the daily gloom we're currently in.

Bias in Google Maps' 'Areas of Interest', and the Impact on Wealth Inequality
--:--

At the Hack Your City Berlin, a small team worked on analysis of the Google Maps feature called 'Areas Of Interest'. Google claims that density of shops and businesses are what primarily guides the algorithm for designating Areas of Interest. Our project showed that there is a bias when it comes to neighborhoods being designated as interesting: neighborhoods with more class privilege, and less ethnic minorities, are more likely to be labeled as interesting, even though they have lower density of businesses than nearby poor or minority neighborhoods. This presentation shows this bias with spatial data and maps. I also will talk about the risks of self strengthening algorithms when it comes to urban demographies, and the possible impacts of this software with regards too segregation and increasing wealth inequality.

putting decentralised trust into compromise-resilient community repositories
--:--

some people develop code. to enable others to use this code, a release needs to be done, and a way to distribute this release. this is the purpose of a community repository. certainly, trust should not be centralised in such a repository. instead a user should be able to verify that the original developer released this piece of code. additionally, the user should be able to verify that they got a fresh view of the repository with all the latest releases without any known security issues.

I'll briefly talk about the threat model I have in mind for such a repository, and some solutions I worked on in the past. not all of it is yet finalised and implemented :) we can discuss the tradeoffs and how other systems solve these issues.

this work is based on tuf http://theupdateframework.github.io/ , extended with TAP8 https://github.com/theupdateframework/taps/blob/tap8/tap8.md , which is inspired by conex https://hannes.nqsb.io/Posts/Conex (see https://ssl.engineering.nyu.edu/blog/2017-05-15-conex for the actual work being presented)

Is basic income the answer to robotization?
--:--

This is not a lecture but rather a discussion. With 5 minute introduction to subject to than further discuss with folks who are interested. Subject: Robotization will probably erode many jobs. So much so that the traditional model of working for wages will no longer hold. An often proposed alternative for the division of wealth is implementing a universal basic income. However, this places power in the hands of corporations and states to decide how much people should receive and under what conditions. An alternative is Universal Basic Divident: a fixed percentage of proceeds generated by robots should flow to the collective.

I Come From the Third-TechnoScape
--:--

Propaganda design workshop (we bring a tee-shirt press) and conversation around the concept of Third-TechnoScape. Tee-shirts, slogans, media invasion tactics, fun with propaganda from the city to social media.

In the shades of capitalist hegemony and ideology, we cast the 3TS to respond to the reductionist market-driven fallacy of "Third Space", between home and the workplace, where social capital can easily be captured and executed while the worker's guard is lowered.
